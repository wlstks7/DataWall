California

California's Immigration

Legal and Illegal Residence

2005 - 2012 | Source

Between 2005 to 2012, California received just under 25% of all unauthorized immigration and almost 27% of legal permanent residents into the United States. California’s unauthorized immigration grew from 2005 until 2008. In 2009, immigration decreased, and 2010’s pre-census data projected a further decrease, but post-census data shows that California underestimated that year. The odd-looking rise in 2010 is a comparison of the January 2010 unauthorized immigration report by the Department of Homeland Security and their amended estimations published in 2012 that estimated the unauthorized population using census data. Hovering the mouse on the left side of 2010 displays pre-census estimation and, on the right side, the post-census estimate. Post-census data reveals a 340,000 person underestimation of unauthorized immigrants in 2010, and the reporting methods seem to have adjusted for this in the years after.

Legal permanent residents generally follow the rise and decline of unauthorized immigration. Legal residents grew from 2006 to 2007, fell during 2008 and 2009, and continued to rise through 2012. Legal immigration peaked in 2007 and shared its lowest point with unauthorized immigration in 2009.

How we found this.

Data of unauthorized immigration from Mexico follows the overall immigration into California expectedly. Legal permanent residents differed from the overall immigration in 2009 - 2012. While overall legal permanent residents grew, legal permanent residents from Mexico declined. Pre and post-census data show an underestimation of 133,328 unauthorized immigrants from Mexico in 2010.

California's Immigration Popularity

Percentage of unauthorized immigration and legal permanent residents from Mexico into California versus the other states.

Guide to finding state popularity.

California received about 25% of all unauthorized immigration and almost 27% of legal permanent residents into the United States from 2005 to 2012. Comparing the yearly increases and decreases in percentage versus the nominal increases and decreases the “popularity” of immigration into California from Mexico can be quantified compared to the other 49 states.

Unauthorized Immigration Popularity
From 2005 to 2007, the percentage decreased and nominal increased. There was more overall immigration into the U.S., but the state experienced a lower total than uniform. In 2008, both the percentage and nominal are both increasing, and the state’s popularity, regardless of the U.S. total immigration, increased. In 2009, both the percentage and nominal decreased, therefore decreasing California’s popularity. Pre-census data shows a continued decrease in popularity, but post-census data shows the opposite. The next year, 2011, shows a decrease in popularity as percentage and nominal where lower. In 2012 there was less overall immigration into the U.S., but the state experienced a growth in popularity.

Legal Permanent Resident Popularity
California’s legal permanent resident immigration is a constant decrease of popularity from 2006 to 2012 regardless of the U.S. total immigration.
