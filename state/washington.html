Washington

Washington's Immigration

Legal and Illegal Residence

2005 - 2012 | Source

Between 2005 and 2012, Washington received about 18% of all unauthorized immigration and about 2% of legal permanent residents into the United States. Washington’s unauthorized immigration increased from 2005 to 2006 and decreased in 2007. From 2007 to 2008, the nominal estimation of immigration remained the same. Immigration decreased from 2008 to 2009. Pre-census data showed a continued decrease, but post-census estimations showed that immigration actually increased and maintained the same nominal numbers in 2011. Immigration continued to increase through 2012. The odd-looking increase in 2010 is a comparison of the January 2010 unauthorized immigration report by the Department of Homeland Security and their amended estimations published in 2012 that estimated the unauthorized population using census data. Hovering the mouse on the left side of 2010 displays pre-census estimation and, on the right side, the post-census estimate. Post-census data reveals a 60,000 person underestimation of unauthorized immigrants in 2010, and the reporting methods seem to have adjusted for this in the years after.

Legal permanent resident immigration into Washington increased from 2006 to 2007 and remained the same nominal count until 2009. From 2009 to 2010, immigration increased again, and, again, remained the same nominal count until 2011. In 2012, immigration increased. Unauthorized immigration peaked in 2006 at 280,000 immigrants. Legal permanent immigration peaked in 2012 also at 280,000.

Immigration From Mexico

Unauthorized immigration and legal permanent residents from Mexico by the nominal count.

How we found this.

Data of unauthorized immigration from Mexico generally follows the overall immigration into Washington except for the two periods of sustained nominal numbers. From 2007 to 2008 and 2010 to 2011, while overall nominal numbers did not increase or decrease, immigration from Mexico increased. Legal permanent residents from 2006 to 2007 decreased slightly while overall immigration increased. While immigration did not increase or decrease in 2007 to 2008, legal permanent residents increased from Mexico and then decreased in 2009 while overall immigration still maintained.

Percentage of unauthorized immigration and legal permanent residents from Mexico into Washington versus the other states.

Guide to finding state popularity.

Washington received about 18% of all unauthorized immigration and about 2% of legal permanent residents into the United States from 2005 to 2012. Comparing the yearly increases and decreases in percentage versus the nominal increases and decreases the “popularity” of immigration into Illinois from Mexico can be quantified compared to the other 49 states.

Unauthorized Immigration Popularity
From 2005 to 2006, popularity increased as percentage and nominal numbers increased. In 2007, nominal and percentage decreased. Nominal numbers increased in 2008, but this was expected compared to the overall immigration into the U.S. In 2009, immigration nominal numbers and percentage decreased. Pre-census data shows a decrease in popularity, but post-census data suggests a rise in popularity and continued to rise through 2012.

Legal Permanent Resident Popularity
From 2006 to 2007, legal permanent residents decreased, but then increased in 2008. From 2009 through 2012, the percentage of immigration into Washington remained the same. The steady rise in legal permanent residents is uniform to the overall amount of legal immigration into the U.S.
