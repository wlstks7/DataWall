Texas

Texas's Immigration

Legal and Illegal Residence

2005 - 2012 | Source

Between 2005 to 2012, Texas received almost 15% of all unauthorized immigration and about 9.7% of legal permanent residents into the United States. Texas’ unauthorized immigration increased from 2005 to 2007. From 2008 to 2009, immigration estimations remained the same. In 2010, pre-census and post-census data both increased from 2009, but the estimation was very close. Immigration increased until 2012. The odd-looking increase in 2010 is a comparison of the January 2010 unauthorized immigration report by the Department of Homeland Security and their amended estimations published in 2012 that estimated the unauthorized population using census data. Hovering the mouse on the left side of 2010 displays pre-census estimation and, on the right side, the post-census estimate. Post-census data reveals a 10,000 person underestimation of unauthorized immigrants in 2010, and the reporting methods seem to have adjusted for this in the years after.

Legal permanent resident immigration into Texas increased from 2006 to 2008, decreased in 2009, and continued to increase through 2012. Unauthorized and legal immigration peaked in 2012 with 1,830,000 unauthorized and 1,310,000 legal permanent residents.

Immigration From Mexico

Unauthorized immigration and legal permanent residents from Mexico by the nominal count.

How we found this.

Data of unauthorized immigration from Mexico does not exactly follow the overall immigration into Texas. Unauthorized immigration constantly increased from 2005 to 2012. Though immigrations estimates increased post-census for general immigration, immigration from Mexico decreased 40,275 immigrants from pre-census estimation. Legal permanent residents also do not exactly follow the general trend of immigration. From 2007 to 2008, legal permanent residents decreased from Mexico while the general legal immigration increased.

Unauthorized Immigration Popularity
From 2005 to 2007, the percentage and nominal both increased. In 2008, immigration decreased, but the state still experienced the expected nominal totals of immigration into the overall immigration into the U.S. From 2009 to 2012, popularity continued to rise through nominal and percentage increase.

Legal Permanent Resident Popularity
From 2006 to 2007, legal permanent resident immigration increased nominally and percentage. In 2008, there was less overall immigration into the U.S., but Texas still experienced a higher total than uniform of immigration. In 2009, nominal and percentage decreased. In 2010 and 2011, there was an expected increase from 2009 as the percentage remained, but the nominal increase id related to the amount of immigration into the U.S. In 2012, percentage and nominal increased.
