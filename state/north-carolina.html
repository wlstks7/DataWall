North Carolina

North Carolina's Immigration

Legal and Illegal Residence

2005 - 2012 | Source

Between 2005 and 2012, North Carolina received about 3% of all unauthorized immigration and 1% of all legal permanent residents into the United States. North Carolina’s unauthorized immigration maintained the same numbers from 2005 to 2006, increased in 2007, and maintained again through 2008. In 2009, immigration decreased and then increased until 2011. Immigration decreased again in 2012. North Carolina is the only of the ten states that did not have a difference in immigration in 2010 from pre-census and post-census data.

Legal permanent residents increased from 2006 to 2008, maintained the same amount in 2009, and increased in 2010. 2011 maintained the same amount of legal residents from 2010 and increased again in 2012.

Immigration From Mexico

Unauthorized immigration and legal permanent residents from Mexico by the nominal count.

How we found this.

Data of unauthorized immigration from Mexico generally follows the overall immigration into North Carolina with one exception. In 2008, overall immigration did not increase, but immigration from Mexico continued to increase. Post-census data reveals that, while overall immigration did not decrease, immigration from Mexico decreased from the pre-census data but still increased from the previous year. Legal permanent residents from Mexico did not follow overall legal immigration as closely. From 2007 to 2008, overall legal immigration increased while legal immigration from Mexico decreased. The decrease continued to 2009 even as overall legal permanent residents increased. From 2010 to 2011, overall legal permanent residents remained, but legal permanent residents from Mexico increased.

North Carolina's Immigration Popularity

Percentage of unauthorized immigration and legal permanent residents from Mexico into North Carolina versus the other states.

Guide to finding state popularity.

North Carolina received about 3% of all unauthorized immigration and 1% of all legal permanent residents into the United States from 2005 to 2012. Comparing the yearly increases and decreases in percentage versus the nominal increases and decreases the “popularity” of immigration into Illinois from Mexico can be quantified compared to the other 49 states.

Unauthorized Immigration Popularity
From 2005 to 2006, there was more immigration into the U.S., but North Carolina experienced less immigration than uniform despite the increase in nominal. In 2007, nominal increased proportionally to the amount of overall immigration into the U.S. In 2008, both nominal and percentage of immigration increased raising its popularity. Though in 2009 nominal decreased, the state maintained its popularity by having the expected total compared to the U.S. total. Pre-census data shows an increase in popularity, but post-census data shows it did not. In 2011, percentage and nominal increased and then both decreased in 2012.

Legal Permanent Resident Popularity
From 2006 to 2007, legal permanent residents increased nominally and percentage. From 2008 to 2009, immigration decreased in proportion to the overall legal permanent resident immigration into the U.S. those years. In 2010, percentage and nominal increased again. From 2011 to 2012, legal immigration slightly increased uniformly compared to overall immigration for those years.
