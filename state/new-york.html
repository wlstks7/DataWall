New York

New York's Immigration

Legal and Illegal Residence

2005 - 2012 | Source

Between 2005 and 2012, New York received about 5% of all unauthorized immigration and 12% of legal permanent residents into the United States. New York’s unauthorized immigration dropped from 2005 to 2006. In 2007 immigration increased and maintained the same amount through 2008 until decreasing in 2009. Pre-census data shows a decrease in unauthorized immigration, but post-census data reveals an increase. Unauthorized immigration then decreased through 2012. The odd-looking increase in 2010 is a comparison of the January 2010 unauthorized immigration report by the Department of Homeland Security and their amended estimations published in 2012 that estimated the unauthorized population using census data. Hovering the mouse on the left side of 2010 displays pre-census estimation and, on the right side, the post-census estimate. Post-census data reveals a 230,000 person underestimation of unauthorized immigrants in 2010, and the reporting methods seem to have adjusted for this in the years after.

Legal permanent residents increased from 2006 to 2007, decreased in 2008, and maintained the same numbers in 2009. From 2009 through 2012, legal permanent residents increased and peaked in 2012. Unauthorized immigration peaked after the census in 2010 when originally it would have been New York’s lowest year of immigration.

Immigration From Mexico

Unauthorized immigration and legal permanent residents from Mexico by the nominal count.

How we found this.

Data of unauthorized immigration from Mexico follows the overall immigration into New York except for the increase from 2007 to 2008 when overall immigration remained the same. Legal permanent residents also follow the overall immigration except for the decrease from 2008 to 2009 when overall immigration remained the same. Pre and post-census data show an underestimation of 123,541 immigrants from Mexico in 2010. In that year, unauthorized and legal immigrants from Mexico almost matched each other.

New York's Immigration Popularity

Percentage of unauthorized immigration and legal permanent residents from Mexico into New York versus the other states.

Guide to finding state popularity.

New York received about 5% of all unauthorized immigration and 12% of legal permanent residents into the United States from 2005 to 2012. Comparing the yearly increases and decreases in percentage versus the nominal increases and decreases the “popularity” of immigration into Illinois from Mexico can be quantified compared to the other 49 states.

Unauthorized Immigration Popularity
From 2005 to 2006, popularity decreased as the percentage and nominal decreased. From 2007 to 2008, nominal and percentage began to increase. 2009 showed a decrease in popularity until 2010 where post-census data showed a sharp jump in popularity. After the census from 2011 to 2012, both the nominal and percentage of unauthorized immigration decreased.

Legal Permanent Resident Popularity
From 2006 to 2007, nominal immigration increased but experienced a lower total than uniform. In 2008, both nominal and percentage decreased continuing to lower its popularity. Though there was less nominal immigration in 2009, New York had a higher percentage of immigration, and the state experienced a higher total than uniform for that year. In 2010, nominal and percentage increased and the increase of nominal in 2011 grew in relation to the percentage. 2012 showed another increase in nominal and percentage.
