Arizona

Arizona's Immigration

Legal and Illegal Residence

2005 - 2012 | Source

Between 2005 to 2012, Arizona received just under 4% of all unauthorized immigration and almost 1.9% of legal permanent residents into the United States. Arizona’s unauthorized immigration grew from 2005 until 2008, and unauthorized immigration continued to fall until 2012 excluding a small growth in 2011 of about 10,000 aliens post-census. The odd-looking drop in 2010 is a comparison of the January 2010 unauthorized immigration report by the Department of Homeland Security and their amended estimations published in 2012 that estimated the unauthorized population using census data. Hovering the mouse on the left side of 2010 displays pre-census estimation and, on the right side, the post-census estimate. Post-census data reveals a 120,000 person overestimation of unauthorized immigrants in 2010, and the reporting methods seem to have adjusted for this in the years after.

During the declining years of unauthorized immigration into Arizona, the population of legal permanent residents grew from 2008 to 2012 and reached its highest in 2012. In the same year, unauthorized immigration was also at its lowest.

Immigration From Mexico

Unauthorized immigration and legal permanent residents from Mexico by the nominal count.

How we found this.

Data of immigrants from Mexico follows the overall immigration into Arizona expectedly. During all years, if legal or unauthorized immigration into Arizona increased or decreased, so did immigration from Mexico. Pre and post-census data show an overestimation of 82,975 unauthorized immigrants from Mexico in 2010.

Arizona's Immigration Popularity

Percentage of unauthorized immigration and legal permanent residents from Mexico into Arizona versus the other states.

Guide to finding state popularity.

Arizona received about 4% of all unauthorized immigration and almost 1.9% of legal permanent residents into the United States from 2005 to 2012. Comparing the yearly increases and decreases in percentage versus the nominal increases and decreases the “popularity” of immigration into Arizona from Mexico can be quantified compared to the other 49 states.

Unauthorized Immigration Popularity
From 2005 to 2006, the percentage decreased and nominal increased. There was more overall immigration into the U.S., but the state experienced a lower total than uniform. From 2007 to 2008, the percentage and nominal count are both increasing, and the state’s popularity, regardless of the U.S. total immigration, increased. In 2009, both the percentage and nominal count decreased, and so did its popularity. Pre-census data indicates a growth in popularity, but post-census estimations show a decrease in immigration popularity. In 2011 Arizona’s popularity increased and, though the nominal count decreased in 2012, maintained the same popularity.

Legal Permanent Resident Popularity
From 2006 to 2007, Arizona’s percentage and nominal count increased, but in 2008 lost some popularity as the percentage and nominal count decreased. In 2009, Arizona’s popularity increased again and maintained its popularity through 2012. Though the population of legal permanent residents continued to grow slightly, Arizona experienced expected nominal totals of immigration in relation to the overall increase of immigration into the U.S.
